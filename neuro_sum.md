# Neuroscience Paper Summary: Technology Roadmap of Bioinspired Computing Hardware

**Paper Title:** Technology Roadmap of Bioinspired Computing Hardware  
**Authors:** Shuang Wang et al. (Large international collaboration)  
**Journal:** ACS Nano  
**Publication Date:** March 2, 2026  
**DOI:** 10.1021/acsnano.5c17087  
**PMID:** 41771048  

## Overview

This comprehensive review paper presents a technology roadmap for bioinspired computing (BIC) hardware, addressing the fundamental hardware bottlenecks that constrain the rapid growth of artificial intelligence (AI). The paper emphasizes the need for interdisciplinary convergence between materials science, device physics, neuroscience, computer science, mathematics, and information science.

## Key Problem Addressed

Current AI development faces significant limitations in computation throughput and energy efficiency due to conventional hardware architectures. Bioinspired computing offers a promising alternative by emulating the intrinsic advantages of biological systems, including:
- Parallelism
- Adaptability  
- Robustness

## Three-Component Framework

The paper categorizes critical challenges into three main components:

### 1. Hardware Foundations
- Examines how biological features inspire BIC hardware design through device physics
- Discusses performance metrics and engineering challenges
- Focuses on neuronal and synaptic devices

### 2. Architectures
- Describes diverse signaling rules and structural organizations in BIC architectures
- Explains how these support specific computational prototypes
- Covers both electronic and photonic BIC chips

### 3. Prototype Realizations
- Presents technological roadmap for expanding BIC hardware functionality
- Emphasizes coordinated advances in devices, architectures, and system demonstrations

## Key Technologies Mentioned

- **Neuromorphic chips**: Brain-inspired computing architectures
- **In-memory computing**: Processing data where it's stored
- **In-sensor computing**: Processing at the sensor level
- **Synaptic devices**: Mimicking biological synapses
- **Retinomorphic devices**: Vision-inspired computing

## Interdisciplinary Approach

The paper stresses that progress in BIC hardware requires convergence of multiple disciplines:
- **Neuroscience**: Understanding biological computation principles
- **Materials Science**: Developing novel materials for BIC devices
- **Device Physics**: Creating hardware that mimics biological functions
- **Computer Science**: Designing efficient algorithms and architectures
- **Mathematics**: Modeling complex biological systems

## Significance

This roadmap paper provides a systematic analysis of frontier issues and latest progress in BIC hardware. It outlines opportunities to accelerate the shift toward high-efficiency AI hardware through bioinspired approaches, potentially overcoming current limitations in computation and energy efficiency.

## Future Outlook

The ongoing convergence of interdisciplinary knowledge highlighted in this paper is expected to help drive the development of next-generation AI hardware that more closely resembles the efficiency and adaptability of biological systems.
